Cardio Options

Before or after lifting · Low-impact · 15–20 min · EDS + POTS friendly

Cardio is optional but encouraged. 15–20 minutes before or after lifting. Keep heart rate moderate — you should be able to hold a conversation. No running, no elliptical. POTS note: start slowly and increase pace gradually. If dizzy, slow down immediately.
Approved Cardio Options

🚶 Incline Treadmill Walk

4–6% incline · 2.8–3.2 mph · 15–20 min. Burns more than flat walking without joint impact. Great POTS option — consistent rhythm helps blood flow regulation.

🚴 Stationary Bike

Moderate resistance · 15–20 min. Seated = easier on joints and POTS. Planet Fitness has upright and recumbent bikes. Recumbent is the safest POTS option.

🪜 StairMaster

Low speed · 10–15 min to start. Builds glutes and legs while elevating heart rate. Hold rails lightly for balance — don't lean on them. Build up slowly.

🚣 Rower

Low resistance · 10–15 min. Full body, low impact. Focus on leg drive — 60% legs, 20% core, 20% arms. Don't hyperextend at finish position.

Cardio Timing
Before liftingGood warm-up effect. Rest 3–5 min before starting weights.
After liftingFinisher style. Helps flush lactic acid. Keeps heart rate elevated.

Notes & Guidelines

EDS · POTS · GLP-1 · Training approach

EDS Guidelines
Tempo2 sec up · 1 sec pause · 3 sec down. Always.
RangeStop 1–2 reps before end range on every exercise.
LoadLight to moderate. Form is the priority for months 1–2.
No stretchingAlready hypermobile — aggressive stretching increases instability.
Pain ruleMuscle burn = good. Joint pain = stop immediately.
POTS Guidelines
HydrateDrink water before and during every session.
Rise slowlyAfter floor exercises, sit up slowly. Wait 30 sec before standing.
Dizzy = stopSit or lie down immediately. Don't push through lightheadedness.
Cardio startAlways begin cardio at lowest setting and ramp up slowly.
GLP-1 Notes
Protein priorityGLP-1 reduces appetite — make sure protein stays high to protect muscle.
Pre-workoutEat something light 60–90 min before training even if not hungry.
Muscle goalLifting while on GLP-1 preserves lean mass during weight loss. This is the right move.
Goal — What Success Looks Like
After 8 weeks of consistent training, Amy should feel stronger, move with more confidence, and see visible muscle definition in her arms, shoulders, back, and legs. Weight on the scale matters less than how clothes fit and how strong she feels. The program is built to complement the GLP-1 — not fight it.
