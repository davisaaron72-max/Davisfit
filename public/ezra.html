Meal 1 — Breakfast (7–8am)
Option A — Egg & Sweet Potato Power Bowl Both
  • 3–4 scrambled eggs (Ezra) / 2–3 eggs (Abram)
  • ½ sweet potato, cubed and pan-roasted in avocado oil
  • Handful of fresh fruit (berries, banana)
  • Drizzle of raw honey if needed for sweetness
Option B — Ground Beef Hash Both
  • 4oz (Ezra) / 3oz (Abram) lean ground beef, seasoned
  • Diced sweet potato or white potato + peppers
  • 2 eggs cooked into the hash
  • Side of fruit
Option C — Corn Tortilla Breakfast Quesadilla Both
  • 2 corn tortillas (naturally gluten-free)
  • Eggs + ground beef or shredded chicken inside

  • Ezra — no cheese; Abram — add cheese if desired

Snack 1 — Mid-Morning (10–10:30am)

  • Hard-boiled eggs (1–2) with a pinch of salt

Rotate options so it stays fresh. Keep snacks simple — prep the night before.

Progression Notes
  • Start at the lower end of listed weights — form first, always.
  • If they hit all reps cleanly for 2 sessions in a row → bump weight one step up.
  • Ezra: focus on posterior chain (hamstrings/glutes) for court explosion. Abram: focus on core stability and lateral power for linebacker movement.
  • No max effort barbell work yet — DB and bodyweight build the foundation safely at their ages.
  • Squat rack can be used for goblet squats with a DB or for box step-ups. Save barbell squats for when they're 13–14+.

Skill Blocks

4 days/week (Mon–Fri) · Can run together or separately · 45–60 min blocks

Ezra Basketball Skill Block — 45–60 min
Block 1 — Ballhandling (10 min)
  • Stationary dribble series: low, high, crossover, behind back, between legs
  • Two-ball dribbling: alternating, simultaneous
  • Dr. Dish ball return cone dribble routes
Block 2 — Shooting (20–25 min via Dr. Dish)
  • Spot shooting: 5 spots × 10 makes each = 50 makes
  • Pull-up off dribble (elbow → wing)
  • Catch-and-shoot off Dish feed at game speed
  • Goal: 200 makes or 300 attempts — track daily
Block 3 — PG Moves (10 min)
  • Attack the rim: hesitation → drive, crossover → drive
  • Pick-and-roll reads (cones as defenders)
  • Floater: left and right hand
  • Pull-back dribble → reset
Block 4 — Conditioning (5–10 min)
  • Suicide runs or 3/4 court sprints × 4
  • Defensive slide — cone to cone × 4
  • Speed ladder — basketball-specific patterns
Abram Football Skill Block — 45–60 min
Block 1 — Footwork (10 min)
  • Speed ladder: linebacker shuffle, crossover, in-out
  • Cone drills: W-drill, T-drill, 5-10-5
  • Lateral explosion: cone to cone → break on signal
Block 2 — LB Fundamentals (20 min)
  • Read and react: Aaron holds cone as "ball carrier," Abram mirrors and breaks
  • Shuffle → downhill attack: 3 steps shuffle, drop step, full speed close
  • Backpedal → break on signal → sprint
  • Tackling form: leverage, drive through, no head-down contact
Block 3 — Catching / Route Running (10 min)
  • Aaron throws routes: out, slant, curl, seam
  • Hands catching — no body catches
  • YAC (yards after catch) — catch then sprint to cone
Block 4 — Speed & Conditioning (10 min)
  • 10-yd burst starts × 6 (stance → explosion)
  • 40-yd runs × 4 — focus on drive phase
  • Hurdle work: lateral, forward

Recovery Protocol

You've got everything you need — cold plunge, sauna, good food. Use them.

❄️ Cold Plunge

  • Best after heavy lower body or full body days
  • Ezra: 2–3 min at 50–55°F
  • Abram: start at 1–2 min, build up
  • Don't use immediately before bed on hard training days
  • Good for inflammation, soreness, mental toughness

🔥 Sauna

  • Best on rest days or post-cold plunge
  • 10–15 min max at their ages
  • Hydrate before and after — water or coconut water
  • Great for muscle relaxation, circulation, sleep quality
  • Don't use when already dehydrated

😴 Sleep

  • Ezra: 9–10 hours — non-negotiable for ASD + athlete
  • Abram: 9–10 hours
  • Consistent bedtime matters more than duration
  • No screens 30–45 min before bed
  • Sleep is when they actually grow and adapt

🥩 Post-Workout Nutrition

  • Eat within 30–45 min after training
  • Protein + carb combo: eggs + rice, meat + potato, etc.
  • Coconut water for electrolyte replenishment
  • No skipping the post-workout meal — especially Sundays after the big training day

🧘 Mobility / Soft Tissue

  • 5 min stretch after every session — hips, hamstrings, calves, shoulders
  • Foam roll quads and IT band on lower body days
  • Hip flexor stretch daily — critical for both sports
  • Chest / shoulder opener for Abram after football contact

📋 Signs to Back Off

  • Persistent joint pain (not muscle soreness) → rest 1–2 days
  • Mood / irritability drop → check sleep and food first
  • Flat effort in skill sessions → likely accumulated fatigue
  • Any re-stinger symptoms for Abram → stop immediately
  • When in doubt, rest wins over grinding
