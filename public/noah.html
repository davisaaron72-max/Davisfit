Treadmill Days

Tuesday & Thursday · Afternoon · 20–30 minutes · No running required

Walking on a slight incline burns more than flat walking and builds your legs at the same time. You can listen to music, a podcast, an audiobook — whatever keeps you on it. The goal is just to keep moving for the full time.

The Basic Walk Protocol

  • Speed: 2.8 – 3.5 mph (whatever feels like a brisk walk)
  • Incline: 4–6% — enough to feel it in your calves and glutes
  • Duration: Start at 20 min, work up to 30 min by week 3
  • No holding the rails — arms swing naturally, posture tall
  • Drink water before you start and keep it nearby
Simple Progression
Week 1–220 min · 4% incline · 2.8–3.0 mphStart here
Week 3–425 min · 5% incline · 3.0–3.2 mphStep up
Week 5–630 min · 5–6% incline · 3.2–3.5 mphCruising
Week 7–830 min · 6% · add 2-min jog interval if you wantOptional push
What to Do While You Walk
🎵 Music playlist 🎧 Podcast or audiobook 📱 YouTube (mount your phone) 🎬 Watch a show

Make it something you actually look forward to. If the treadmill time becomes entertainment time, you'll stop dreading it.

The One Rule

If you miss a day, don't try to make it up. Just show up the next scheduled day. Consistency over time beats perfection every week. Missing one day doesn't break anything — quitting does.

Recovery

How you treat your body between sessions matters as much as the sessions themselves.

Sleep — Most Important Thing on This List

At 14, your body does most of its repair and growth while you sleep. Cutting sleep short slows progress, tanks your mood, and makes everything harder.

Hydration

At 5'7" and 140 lbs, Noah should aim for ~70–80 oz of water per day — more on workout days. Water first. No soda, no juice. Unsweetened drinks are fine.

The soreness you feel in the first week or two is normal. It means your body is responding. It gets easier — and then it starts to feel good. That's when the habit locks in.
